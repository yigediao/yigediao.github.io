Research Overview

My research focuses on vision-driven closed-loop robotic systems for real-world manipulation and assembly, with an emphasis on construction-scale tasks using large industrial robots. I develop and validate 3D perception algorithm based on RGB-D sensing and point cloud geometry, and tightly integrate perception, state estimation, decision-making, and execution to enable robots to operate under geometric uncertainty, occlusion, and environmental variability. My work combines high-fidelity digital twins and real robotic workstations, ensuring that methods transfer reliably from simulation to physical deployment.

Research Interests

  • Embodied perception for robotic manipulation in the physical world
  • 3D scene understanding for action, using RGB-D data, geometry, and object-level reasoning
  • Closed-loop visual servoing and adaptive control for precision-critical tasks
  • Robotic assembly under uncertainty, addressing symmetry, tolerances, and imperfect components
  • Perception-planning-execution co-design for deployable robotic systems
  • Digital twins and sim-to-real pipelines for scalable robotic development
